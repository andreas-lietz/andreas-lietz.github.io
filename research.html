Papers

The Model-Theoretic Covering-Reflection Property, Joel D. Hamkins, Nai-Chung Hou, Andreas Lietz and Farmer Schlutzenberg, in preparation. Abstract

The Model-Theoretic Covering Reflection Property holds at a cardinal $\kappa$ if for any first order structure $B$ in a countable language, there is a structure $A$ of size ${<}\kappa$ so that $B$ is covered by isomorphic copies of $A$ which are elementary in $B$. To be precise, we demand $B=\bigcup\{\mathrm{ran(j)}\mid j\colon A\rightarrow B\text{ elementary}\}$. We introduce a new large cardinal between almost huge and huge in consistency strength and show that the least such cardinal is exactly the least cardinal which exhibits the Model-Theoretic Covering-Reflection Property.

Infinite Hat Problems and MInA Cardinals, Andreas Lietz and Jeroen Winkel, in preparation. Preprint, Abstract

We introduce and solve infinite variants of the popular hat guessing logic problems where the non-existence of a winning strategy for the logicians has large cardinal consistency strength.

More Instances of $\mathrm{MM}^{++}\Rightarrow(*)$, Andreas Lietz, in preparation. Abstract

We introduce a number of forcing axioms of similar strength as $MM^{++}$ and prove that they imply versions of $(*)$ with relatives of $\mathbb P_{\mathrm{max}}$ taking on the role of $\mathbb P_{\mathrm{max}}$. This includes for example a forcing axiom implying $\mathbb{S}_{\mathrm{max}}-(*)$ and forcing axioms conditioned on $\mathfrak b=\aleph_1$, $\mathfrak d=\aleph_1$ respectively.

Forcing "\(\mathrm{NS}_{\omega_1}\) is \(\omega_1\)-Dense” from Large Cardinals., Andreas Lietz, Submitted. Preprint, Abstract

We answer a question of Woodin by showing that assuming the existence of an inaccessible cardinal $\kappa$ which is a limit of ${<\kappa}$-supercompact cardinals, there is a stationary set preserving forcing $\mathbb P$ so that $V^{\mathbb P}\models``\mathrm{NS}_{\omega_1} \text{ is }\omega_1\text{-dense}"$. We introduce a new forcing axiom $\mathrm{QM}$ of roughly the same strength as $\mathrm{MM}^{++}$ which implies $``\mathrm{NS}_{\omega_1} \text{ is }\omega_1\text{-dense}"$.

An Iteration Theorem for $\omega_1$-Preserving Forcings, Andreas Lietz, Submitted. Preprint, Abstract

We provide an iteration theorem which shows that a broad range of $\mathrm{RCS}$-iteration of $\omega_1$-preserving forcings preserve $\omega_1$. The argument introduces a new regularity property for $\omega_1$-preserving forcings called respectfulness. Respectfulness is roughly the right version of semiproperness for general $\omega_1$-preserving forcings.

The Axiom of Choice in the $\kappa$-Mantle, Andreas Lietz, Submitted. Preprint, Abstract

We answer a question of Usuba by constructing models of $\mathrm{ZFC}$ in which the $\kappa$-mantle $\mathbb{M_\kappa}$ is not a model of $\mathrm{ZFC}$ for some cardinal $\kappa$. Here, the $\kappa$-mantle is the intersection of all grounds that extend to $V$ via a forcing of size ${<\kappa}$. We can arrange that such a $\kappa$ is a Mahlo cardinal, while Schindler has proved that $\mathbb M_\kappa\models\mathrm{ZFC}$ if $\kappa$ is measurable.

Further Writing

A Journey Guided by the Stars, my dissertation. PDF Abstract

We answer a question of Woodin by showing that “$\mathrm{NS}_{\omega_1}$ is $\omega_1$- dense” holds in a stationary set preserving extension of any universe with a cardinal $\kappa$ which is a limit of ${<\kappa}$-supercompact cardinals. We do so by introducing a forcing axiom we call $Q$-Maximum, prove it consistent from large cardinals and show that it implies the version of Woodin’s $(\ast)$-axiom for $\mathbb{Q}_{\mathrm{max}}$. Along the way we produce a number of other new instances of Asperó-Schindler’s result $\mathrm{MM}^{++}\Rightarrow(\ast)$. In the second part, we show that the $\kappa$-mantle, i.e. the intersection of all grounds which extend to $V$ via forcing of size ${<}\kappa$, may fail to be a model of $\mathrm{AC}$ for various instances of $\kappa$. This answers a question of Usuba.

Forcing $(*)$ from less than a Supercompact, Andreas Lietz and Ralf Schindler. PDF Abstract

We show that assuming a proper class of Woodin cardinals and an inaccessible limit of $\kappa^{++}$-supercompact cardinals $\kappa$, there is a stationary set preserving partial order $\PP$ which forces $(\ast)$. The partial supercompact cardinals are used only to make sure that the Asperó-Schindler $(\ast)$-forcing is semiproper often enough.

$(\prod_{i<\kappa}x(i), \leq^\ast)$ and its bounding number. PDF Abstract

For inaccessible cardinals $\kappa$, we investigate the relationship between $(\prod_{i<\kappa} x(i), \leq^\ast)$ and $(\kappa^\kappa,\leq^\ast)$ for some $x : \kappa \rightarrow \mathrm{Lim}\cap\kappa$. To be precise, we analyse when there is a continuous cofinal embedding $\pi:(\prod_{i<\kappa} x(i), \leq^\ast) \rightarrow (\kappa^\kappa,\leq^\ast) $. We show that, under some natural constraints on $x$, this question is equivalent to whether Hechler forcing on $\prod_{i<\kappa} x(i)$ adds a $\kappa^\kappa$-dominating function and also to the existence of a certain type of $\kappa$-Aronszajn tree. We also prove that there is a ${<\kappa}$-distributive forcing that adds a $\kappa$-Aronszajn tree of this type. It follows that the existence of such an embedding on $\kappa$ is independent of the theory $\mathrm{ZFC}+``\kappa \text{ is inaccessible}"$, relative to a weakly compact cardinal. Finally, we discuss a strategy to produce a model where $\mathfrak{b}(\prod_{i<\kappa} x(i),\leq^\ast)>\mathfrak{b}_{\kappa}$.

The Problem with Exercise 34.5, PDF Abstract

We show that the characterization of the complete Boolean algebra generated by $\Col(\omega,{<}\kappa)$ as presented in Jech's Set Theory Exercise 34.5 is not quite correct. We also provide a corrected characterization.

Set-Theoretic Geology, my Master thesis written at the university of Bonn. PDF Abstract

This thesis surveys a number of results in Set-Theoretic Geology, particularly Usuba's breakthroughs. It is proved that consistently, there is a proper class of supercompact cardinals in the mantle while no model in the generic multiverse of $V$ has any supercompact cardinal.
